🎯 Strategic Product Thinking

Product decisions aren't just tactical—they're strategic choices that shape competitive position, user trust, and long-term value. Below are real decisions from my products where I chose to optimize for strategy (trust, ethics, user agency) over short-term metrics (growth, automation, efficiency).

Each decision shows how I approach tradeoffs: analyze options, understand downstream impact, and commit to choices aligned with product strategy—not just convenient features.

🎯 Decision: No Hiring Scores in Resume Copilot

Context: Hiring AI tools often provide numeric "fit scores" (e.g., 72% match).

Decision made: Avoided all numeric scoring. Used qualitative indicators (Strong/Developing/Early) with explanations.

Why:

  • Numeric scores imply false precision
  • Users may over-trust numbers without understanding reasoning
  • Hiring decisions require context beyond resumes

Outcome: Higher trust, explainability, and responsible AI design.

⚖️ Tradeoff: Automation vs. Human Judgment

Context: Could have built automated hire/reject recommendations.

Decision made: Designed as decision-support tool, not automation. AI supports—doesn't replace—human judgment.

Tradeoff:

  • Gave up: Full automation, faster throughput
  • Gained: User trust, legal safety, ethical responsibility

Result: Product positioned for long-term adoption with responsible AI practices.

🔍 Feature Scoping: What NOT to Build

Context: Could have added ATS integrations, resume ranking, job auto-apply.

Decision made: Explicitly excluded these features in V1.

Reasoning:

  • Adds complexity without improving core user value
  • Increases privacy, bias, and legal risks
  • Distracts from validating core product hypothesis

Impact: Faster MVP validation, clearer product focus.
