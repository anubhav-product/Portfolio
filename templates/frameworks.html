🧠 Strategic Framework Approach

Frameworks aren't just tools—they're strategic thinking structures. They force clarity, surface tradeoffs, and make decisions defensible to stakeholders. My approach combines problem-solving rigor (MECE, issue trees) with execution (RICE, roadmapping) and AI-specific considerations (trust, explainability).

Why this matters: In ambiguous environments (strategy, AI products, transformation), frameworks create structure. They're the difference between scattered thinking and coherent strategy.

🎯 Problem Framing Canvas

How I validate whether a problem is worth solving

1. Problem Statement

Who experiences this? What exactly is broken? When/where does it happen?

Example: "PMs spend 3+ hours manually analyzing product decisions without structured frameworks"
2. Impact Assessment

How many users? How often? What's the cost of inaction?

Quantify: frequency, severity, market size
3. Root Cause

Why does this problem exist? 5 Whys analysis.

Surface systemic issues, not symptoms
4. Success Criteria

How will we know it's solved? Define measurable outcomes.

Leading indicators + lagging metrics

⚖️ Feature Prioritization: RICE + Trust

Modified RICE framework with AI-specific trust dimension

✅ Real Application: AI Interview Copilot Feature Prioritization

Applied RICE+Trust to decide between automated scoring vs. qualitative indicators. Scoring had high Reach/Impact but low Trust score (opaque AI). Qualitative indicators scored 8/10 on Trust. Result: Built explainable system, gained user confidence.

Factor What I Evaluate Score (1-10) AI-Specific Considerations
Reach How many users affected per period? % of user base Consider both direct users and downstream impact
Impact How much does it improve their experience? Massive (3) → Minimal (0.25) Does it increase or decrease user agency?
Confidence How confident am I in estimates? High (100%) → Low (50%) For AI: Model reliability, edge case coverage
Effort Person-months required Total team effort Include model training, testing, monitoring setup
Trust Does this increase user trust in AI? 10 (explainable) → 1 (black box) My addition: Penalize opaque automation
Priority Score: (Reach × Impact × Confidence × Trust) / Effort

💰 BCG Growth-Share Matrix

Portfolio analysis for product/market prioritization

How I use it: Categorize products/features by market growth rate vs. relative market share to determine investment strategy and cash flow expectations.

Cash Cows

High share, Low growth

Mature products generating profit. Milk them for cash. Optimize operations, not innovation.

Dogs

Low share, Low growth

Weak market position, limited upside. Consider divesting or repositioning.

Stars

High share, High growth

Winners requiring investment. Protect market position, expand aggressively.

Question Marks

Low share, High growth

High potential but uncertain. Decide: Double down or divest. Requires selective investment.

🏆 Porter's Five Forces

Industry attractiveness and competitive intensity analysis

How I use it: Assess market structure and competitive dynamics before entering a market or launching a product. Identifies where competitive pressure is highest.

1. Supplier Power

How much can suppliers increase prices or reduce quality? Few suppliers = high power.

2. Buyer Power

How easily can customers switch? Many alternatives = high buyer power.

3. Threat of Substitutes

Are there alternative solutions? Better alternatives = higher threat.

4. Threat of New Entrants

How easy is it for competitors to enter? Low barriers = high threat.

5. Competitive Rivalry

Intensity of competition among existing players. High rivalry = commoditization pressure.

🔗 McKinsey 7S Framework

Organizational alignment for strategy execution

How I use it: Diagnose organizational readiness for strategy. Strategy alone isn't enough—systems, people, and structure must align.

Strategy

What is the competitive advantage? Where do we compete? Against whom?

Structure

Is organizational design supporting strategy? Can decisions move fast?

Systems

Do processes support strategy? Are incentives aligned?

Skills

Do we have capabilities to execute? Gaps require hiring/training.

Staff

Right people in right roles? Culture fit for strategy?

Style

Leadership approach. Does culture support change?

Shared Values

Core purpose and mission. Why does the organization exist? Drives everything.

🎯 MECE Structuring

Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive problem breakdown

How I use it: Break complex problems into non-overlapping components that cover all possibilities. Eliminates blind spots and ensures structured analysis.

Example: Why is user acquisition slowing?

  • Top-of-funnel (TOFU): Fewer people aware of product?
    • Marketing spend down? Brand awareness declining?
  • Middle-of-funnel (MOFU): Fewer conversions at signup?
    • Product quality issues? Messaging not resonating?
  • Bottom-of-funnel (BOFU): Lower retention/repeat purchases?
    • Churn increased? Product-market fit weakened?

Benefit: Now investigation is systematic. No overlap (TOFU ≠ MOFU ≠ BOFU). Complete coverage (all acquisition factors covered).

⚖️ Strategic Trade-off Analysis

Evaluating strategic choices with explicit gains/losses

How I use it: Every strategic choice has winners and losers, advantages and costs. Making trade-offs explicit prevents hidden regrets later.

Option A: Vertical Integration

Gain: Control quality, reduce dependency, capture margin

Cost: High capex, operational complexity, slower pivots

Option B: Partnership Model

Gain: Fast scaling, lower risk, focus on core

Cost: Less control, margin sharing, dependency risk

Option C: Hybrid Approach

Gain: Balanced control + speed, learning from both models

Cost: Complexity, coordination overhead, unclear accountability

Key Insight: No option is "best"—only best for your current situation. Decision depends on capital, risk appetite, and competitive timing.

🎪 Competitive Positioning

Market positioning and differentiation strategy

How I use it: Define clear competitive position—what you do uniquely, who you're for, why they should choose you over alternatives.

Cost Leadership

Compete on price. Requires operational excellence, scale, efficiency. Risk: race to bottom.

Differentiation

Unique features/quality. Command premium. Risk: premium positioning may not stick if core value unclear.

Focus/Niche

Dominate specific segment. Deep expertise. Risk: niche may be too small or get disrupted.
