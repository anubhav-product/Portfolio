üéØ Problem Framing Canvas

How I validate whether a problem is worth solving

1. Problem Statement

Who experiences this? What exactly is broken? When/where does it happen?

Example: "PMs spend 3+ hours manually analyzing product decisions without structured frameworks"
2. Impact Assessment

How many users? How often? What's the cost of inaction?

Quantify: frequency, severity, market size
3. Root Cause

Why does this problem exist? 5 Whys analysis.

Surface systemic issues, not symptoms
4. Success Criteria

How will we know it's solved? Define measurable outcomes.

Leading indicators + lagging metrics

üîç Product Analysis Sample: Grammarly

Showing how I analyze products using structured frameworks

Problem It Solves

User Pain: Writing clarity and grammar errors hurt professional credibility.

Why it matters: 73% of employers reject candidates for poor writing (LinkedIn study).

What Makes It Work
  • Inline feedback: Real-time suggestions without breaking flow
  • Explainability: Shows why a suggestion matters
  • User control: Accept/reject each suggestion individually
  • Freemium model: Free tier builds trust, premium adds value
Strategic Risks
  • Over-reliance on AI ‚Üí users lose writing skills
  • Privacy concerns with document analysis
  • Competitor pressure (Microsoft Editor, Notion AI)
  • Tone suggestions risk feeling prescriptive
What I'd Improve

Opportunity: Add "writing patterns" dashboard showing user's common mistakes over time.

Why: Shifts from reactive correction to proactive learning. Increases retention by making users better writers, not just dependent on AI.

Metric to track: % of users showing error reduction over 30 days.
